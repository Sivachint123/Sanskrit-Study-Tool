1. Fundamentals — वर्णमाला & Letters

Basic Concepts
वाक्यम्→A fundamental form of communication
पदं→Meaningful group of words
वर्ण→Sounds (letters)
क्रियापदम् (Kriyapadam)→Verb
नामपदम् (Namapadam)→Noun

अक्षरम् (Aksharam)

That which is indestructable and all-prevailing through its meaning

न क्षायते न क्षरति इति वा अक्षरम्

Varnahah / वर्णि: — Vowels/consonants that you cannot split further (e.g. र् आ म् अ :)

Varnamala — How Many Letters?
  • 48 letters from Matrikamale (Mother Upadeshum)
  • 64 = 56 Svarahs + Vyanjanani, 4 Ayogavaha, 4 Yamaha
  • In Vedam there are longer Svarahs (Pluta forms)
Vyanjanani — व्यञ्जनानि (Consonants)
व्यञ्जनानि→ Consonants are those letters that cannot be pronounced independently

Mrudu Vyanjanani — मृदुव्यञ्जनानि (Soft Consonants)

3rd and 4th letters of each varga:

ग घ ज झ ड ढ द ध ब भ य र ल व ह

Karkasha Vyanjanani — कर्कशव्यञ्जनानि (Hard Consonants)

1st and 2nd letters of each varga:

क ख च छ ट ठ त थ प फ

अयोगवाहाः→ Letters which are not Svarahs or Vyanjanani

Yamaha — यमाः
Yamavarna Rule
If any of the 4 letters of the varga comes in front of the 5th varna, a similar letter is added as per the rules of Shiksha called Yamevarna.

The 5th varna of each varga: ङ् ञ् ण् न् म्

The 4 letters are: क ख ग घ

अग्निः → अग्ग्निः पलिक्नी Really only used in Vedam (Rig Veda Prathi Shaakya)

2. Svarahs — स्वराः (Vowels)

स्वराः→ स्वयं राजन्ते इति स्वराः — Those which exist on their own

Shlokam — Amount of time for each vowel

3. Word Structure — Padam · Sarvanamani · Dhatu

Padam — पदम्

All words = Suptigantam Padam

पदम् (Padam)
सुबन्तम् (Subantam)
= नामपदम् (Namapadam)
All nouns: Ramaha, Phalam…		 तिङन्तम् (Tigantam)
= क्रियापदम् (Kriyapadum)
All verbs: Khadati, Gacchati…
Equation
पदम् = प्रकृतिः + प्रत्ययः
Padam = Prakrithi + Prathyaya

रामस्य → राम + स्य (Pratipadika + Prathyaya)
अस्ति → अस् + ति (As + Ti)
Sarvanamani — सर्वनामानि (Pronouns)
सर्वनामानि→ The words which are used in the place of nouns are called Sarvanamani
नामपदस्य स्थाने उपयुज्यमानाः शब्दाः सर्वनामानि इति उच्यते

5. Vibhaktis — विभक्तयः (Case Suffixes)

Subantam = Pratipadikam + Sup-Prathyaya

This can be: Sarvanamani, Visheshani, Taddhitantaha, Krdantaha, and Samasaha

Vibhakti is a set of suffixes which describe the various situations of the noun.

रामः → राम + ः
1
Prathama Vibhakti — प्रथमा विभक्तिः
Nominative · Subject case

Used for the doer (subject) of the action.

रामः फलं खादति Rama (subject) eats the fruit
2
Dvithiya Vibhakti — द्वितीया विभक्तिः
Accusative · Object / Destination case

Used for the object (receiver) or destination.

रामः फलम् खादति Rama eats the fruit (Kim khadati? → Phalam = Dvithiya)
Special Rule — Avyayaha (indeclinables) that take Dvithiya
SanskritMeaning
अभितःBesides (in 4 directions)
परितःSurrounding (in all 4 directions)
समया / निकशाNear
हाExclamation of sorrow
प्रतिTowards

These are avyayaha — they don't change form for gender, number…
When used in a sentence, the corresponding noun will be in Dvithiya Vibhakti.

Special Rule #2
उभयतः→Both sides
सर्वतः→Everywhere
धिक्→Fie upon
धिक् इमान् कौरवान्Fie upon these Kauravas
Special Rule #3 — Prithak / Vina / Nana (Without)
पृथक् / विना / नाना→Without (use Dvithiya)
रामं पृथक् जगत् नास्ति / रामं विना / रामं नाना Without Rama there is no world
Special Rule #4 — Antara / Antharena
अन्तरा→In Between
अन्तरेण→Without
त्वां मां च अन्तरा हरिः अस्ति Hari is present between me and you हरिम् अन्तरेण न सुखम् There is no happiness without Hari

Dvithiya Shlokas

वासुदेव सुतम् देवम् कंसचानूरमर्दनम्
देवकीपरमानन्दम् कृष्णम् वन्दे जगद्गुरुम्
अच्युतम् केशवम् रामनारायणम् कृष्णदामोदरम्
वासुदेवं हरिम् श्रीधरम् माधवम्
गोपिकावल्लभम् जानकीनायकम् रामभन्द्रम् भजे
3
Trithiya Vibhakti — तृतीया विभक्तिः
Instrumental case · The means by which action is done

The instrument that helps you achieve an action is called करण.

बालः अन्नम् चमसेन खादति Boy eats rice with a spoon

कः खादति? → बालः (Prathama) किम् खादति? → अन्नम् (Dvithiya) केन/कया खादति? → चमसेन (Trithiya)

Panini Rule (न → ण)
If any one of the र् / ष् letters is followed immediately by न् within the word, it gets changed to ण्
षष् + नाम् → षष्णाम् Ramena: र् + आ + म् + ए + न् + अ → न् → ण् (because Svaraha, Pavarga, Nimnita) Harina, Vrkshena → न् becomes ण्; Devena → stays न् (no र/ष)

Ku varga = क् ख् ग् घ् ङ्; Pa varga = प् फ् ब् भ् म्

Upasargahs = Prefix in front of Dhatu (~20 Upasargahs)

Special Rule #1 — Adverbs with Trithiya
भावसुचकानाम् शब्दानां तृतीया विभक्तिः
Trithiya is used when adverbs are describing the action.
सः परिश्रमेण कृषिं करोतिHe does farm work with endeavor बालः संतोषेण मात्रं खादितवान्Boy eats sweets happily
Special Rule #2 — सह / साधम् / समम् / साकम् (Along with)
When these words are used, Trithiya Vibhakti should be used.
रामेण सह सीता वनं अगच्छत्Sita went to forest with Rama कृष्णेन साकं अर्जुनः युद्धाय अगच्छत्Arjuna went to battle with Krishna द्रौपद्या समं सर्वे पाण्डवाः सन्तिAll the Pandavas are with Draupadi
Special Rule #3 — पृथक् / विना / नाना (Without)
When these words are used, Trithiya can also be used (Dvithiya or Trithiya both acceptable).
तेन विना तृणमपि न चलतिWithout him the grass also doesn't move
Special Rule #4 — Cause-Effect in Trithiya
When there is a cause and effect relation between two words, the word signifying the cause will be in Trithiya.
गोविन्दस्य आगमनेन सः संतुष्टः He is pleased by the arrival of Govinda. Cause→arrival of Govinda; Effect→Pleasure अहम् उदरवेदनया बालां न अगच्छम् I didn't go to school because of stomach pain. Cause→Stomach Pain; Effect→Not going
Special Rule #5 — अलम् (Prohibition)
When used in prohibition, use Trithiya.
हे बालाः! कोलाहलेन अलम्Oh Boys! Enough of making noise अलं कलहेनEnough of quarrel
4
Chaturthi Vibhakti — चतुर्थी विभक्तिः
Dative case · The receiver in an action of giving
यत्र दानक्रिया अस्ति, तत्र दानस्वीकर्तुः चतुर्थीविभक्तिः भवति
In the action of giving, the receiver is in Chaturthi Vibhakti.
बाला भिक्षुकाय अन्नं दद्यातिThe girl gives food to the beggar वैद्यः रुग्णाय औषधिं यच्छतिThe doctor gives medicine to the diseased person
Special Rule #1 — Emotional verbs use Chaturthi
कुध्यति, असूयति, ईष्यति, दुह्यति
Angry, Envy, Jealous, Treachery
रामः रावणाय कुध्यतिRama is angry with Ravana कंसः कृष्णाय असूयतिKamsa is envious of Krishna राक्षसाः मनुष्येभ्यः ईष्यन्तेRakshasas are jealous of human beings
Special Rule #2 — रोचते (Likes)
When Rochate is used, the person who likes will be in Chaturthi.
रामाय मधुरं रोचतेRama likes sweets मह्यं संस्कृतं रोचतेI like Sanskrit
Special Rule #3 — नमः / स्वस्ति / स्वाहा / स्वधा / अलम् / वषट्
WordMeaningExample
नमःSalutationदेवाय नमः → Salutation to Devas
स्वस्तिBest wishesप्रजाभ्यः स्वस्ति → Best wishes to the people
स्वाहाHomamअग्नये स्वाहा
स्वधाPitru Karyanपितृभ्यः स्वधा
अलम्Competentकृष्णः कंसाय अलम् → Krishna is enough (to win) Kamsa
वषट्Mantramइन्द्राय वषट्
Special Rule #4 — गत्यर्थ / कर्मपदात् (Going-type words)
When using going-type words, you can use Dvithiya or Chaturthi.
रामः वनाय व्रजतिRama goes to forest
5
Panchami Vibhakti — पञ्चमी विभक्तिः
Ablative case · Separation from something

Object which is separated from something is in Panchami.

फलं वृक्षात् पततिPhalam falls from tree

Generally these Kriyapadams are in Panchami: पतति, गच्छति/आ, आनयति
When you ask कस्मात् / कुतः the answer is in Panchami.

बालः ग्रन्थालयात् गच्छतिThe Boy goes from the Library बालिकाः शालायाः आगच्छन्तिThe girls come from the School भगीरथः गङ्गां स्वर्गात् आनयतिBhagiratha brings Ganga from Svarga त्वं गृहात् कुत्र गच्छसि?Where do you go from the house? बालः अयोध्यायाः मथुरां गच्छतिThe Boy goes from Ayodya to Mathura
Special Rule #1 — तः (Tasil/तः Pratyayuha)
Any namapadam whether Pum, Stri, Napum or different Vachanas, the addition of तः makes it Panchami.
GenderEkaDviBahu
Pumआत्आभ्याम्एभ्यः
Striयाः—आभ्यः
Napumआत्—एभ्यः

All → तः

Special Rule #2 — Source of Fear in Panchami

Words: भयम्, भीतिः, भीतः, भीः, बभेति, त्रस्यति, रक्षति, पाति

बालः व्याघ्रात् बिभेतिBoy is scared of the tiger सः बालं सिंहात् रक्षमतेHe protects the boy from the lion श्री रामः राक्षसेभ्यः महर्षिगणं रक्षितवान्Shri Rama saved the Maharishis from Rakshasas (cause of fear: Rakshasas)
Special Rule #3 — पूर्व / पर (Before / After)
Something happens after or before something.
चैत्रः वैशाखात् पूर्वःChaitra is before Vaishakha रामः लक्ष्मणात् पूर्वः स्नानात् पूर्वं दन्तधावनं करोतिBefore Snanam I brush my teeth
Special Rule #4 — ऋते / विना (Than / Without) — contextual meaning
जलात् ऋते/विना मीनाः न जीवन्ति रामात् ऋते/विना कः रक्षति भगवत्याः ऋते/विना शरणं नास्ति There is no other resort than Bhagavan
Special Rule #5 — बहिः (Outside)
गृहात् बहिः उद्यानम् अस्तिOutside the house there is a park जलात् बाह्यः मीनः न जावतिOutside the water, fish can't survive
Special Rule #6 — River Origin
Where the rivers originate from, the place where it originates is in Panchami.
गङ्गा हिमालयात् प्रभवति
6
Shashti Vibhakti — षष्ठी विभक्तिः
Genitive case · Relationship between 2 nouns

The relation between 2 nouns should use Shashti Vibhakti.

7
Saptami Vibhakti — सप्तमी विभक्तिः
Locative case · Location / Time / In, On, At

Used to denote location, time, in/on/at.
When you ask कुत्र (Where) the answer should be in Saptami.

Special Rule #1 — कदा (When)
When you ask कदा (When) something happened, answer is in Saptami.
अहम् प्रातःकाले देवं नमामि बालः कास्मिन् दिने किं पठति बालः सोमवासरे रामायणम् पाठति
Special Rule #2 — विषयहा (Expressing Emotion in an Object)
Expressing emotion in an object / something — the subject matter is in Saptami.
ध्रुवस्य नारायणे महती भक्तिःDhruva has a lot of Devotion in Narayana मम व्याकरणे इच्छा अस्तिI have interest in Vyakaranam श्रीकृष्णस्य पाण्डवेषु प्रीतिः आसीत्Krishna had love for Pandavas
Special Rule #3 — Nirdana Saptami / Shasti (Best/Worst from a group)
Amongst a set of people/objects/animals/group, we separate one thing and describe it as best or worst. Use Saptami or Shashti.
धनुर्धाराणाम् / धनुर्धारेषु रामः श्रेष्ठःRama is the best of bow users वेदानाम् / वेदेषु ऋग्वेदः प्रथमःIn the Vedas Rigvedam is the first प्राणिनाम् / प्राणिषु गजः बृहत्तमःAmongst the Animals elephant is huge नदानाम् / नदीषु गङ्गा पवित्राAmong the rivers Ganga is Pavitra
Special Rule #4 — सति (When an action happens simultaneously)
Two actions — one action helps to remember the other action. The word सति is added to the main action.
रामे राज्यम् प्रशासति सति, जनाः सर्वे सुखिन आसन् When Rama was living (ruling), people lived happily उढ़ते सूर्ये सति, अन्धकारः नष्टः The darkness dispelled when the Sun Rose
Q: कदा अन्धकारः नष्टः? When was the darkness removed? A: उढ़ते सूर्ये When the Sun Rose

6. Visheshanam — विशेषणम् (Adjectives)

विशेषणम्→ An adjective is a word that describes a noun
Definition
यत् नामपदस्य वैशिष्ट्यं बोधयति तत् विशेषणम्
यस्य वैशिष्ट्यम् उच्यते तत् विशेष्यम् = The noun that is described is called Visheshya.
कृशः बालकः1 — Skinny Boy दीर्घः दण्डः2 — Long Stick स्थूलः गजः3 — Fat Elephant

Two Functions of Visheshana

Svarupa Kathanam (Describing intrinsic nature)
In the case of describing the svarupaha of the namapadam.
Vyavarthaka Visheshanam (Differentiating)
In this case it helps differentiate the namapadam from the others — differentiates the long stick from the short.
Rule — Matching Lingam/Vachanam/Vibhakti
Whatever is the Lingam, Vachanam, Vibhakti of the Visheshya (noun), the same Lingam, Vibhakti, and Vachanam is used for Visheshana (adjective/adverb).

Types of Visheshana

TypeDescriptionExample
Gona Vachakani Describing Guna (quality) धार्मिकः पुरुषः / स्तः पुष्पम् (Sita)
Parimana Vachakani Quantity / measure ईषद् अन्नम् (Serve me little Rice)
Sanketha Bodhakani Identifying this/that (Sarvanamam in front of noun = Visheshana) इयं सीता (This Sita)
Sankhya Bodhakani Numbers एकं ब्रह्मा (One Brahma)

All of these can be Visheshnas.

Sarvanama → अन्या बाचा Avyayaha → काश्चित् पुरुषः Kiduntaha → वर्धमानः पुरुषः (growing) Thadbhita → दाशरथी रामः (Rama, son of Dasharata) → Suffix added

This concept can be extended to verbs as well — these are Kriya-Visheshanes (Adverbs)

मन्दं गच्छतिGoes slowly

7. Avyayani — अव्ययानि (Indeclinables)

अव्ययानि→ Avyaya is that which remains the same in all three Lingas, in all 7 Vibhaktis, and in all three Vachanams and which does not change.
